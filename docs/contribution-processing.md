# How contributed expertise becomes AI answers

This document gives a high-level overview of how contributed expertise — whether shared manually through documents and links, or dynamically through APIs and MCP servers — can ultimately be used and cited inside Askeal’s AI answers.

This document intentionally stays high level and does not describe the internal mechanisms that constitute Askeal’s competitive advantage.

---

## 1. Validation & retrieval

Every contribution is verified before being used in an AI answer.

### Validation

Contributions are evaluated on three dimensions:

| Dimension | Validation goal |
| --- | --- |
| **Relevance** | Does the contribution meaningfully help answer cybersecurity-related requests? |
| **Structure** | Is the content understandable, usable, and properly formatted? |
| **Safety & compliance** | Is the content safe and appropriate to surface? |

Only validated contributions can be used by the answering AI.

This helps ensure:
- high quality across contributor profiles;
- rejection of unsafe, generic, low-quality, or overly promotional content.

### Retrieval

When a user asks a question, Askeal may determine that contributor expertise is relevant.

Depending on the contribution type:

- **Manual contributions** are retrieved from Askeal’s indexed knowledge base.
- **Automated contributions** are queried live through contributor APIs or MCP servers.

A few important points:

- Retrieval spans all contributor profiles by default and is dynamically narrowed based on multiple factors such as relevance, quality, expertise, freshness, and response time.
- Retrieval can also be narrowed to a specific contributor profile, either by Askeal’s AI or explicitly by the user.

---

## 2. Source attribution in AI answers

When contributor expertise is used inside an answer, Askeal displays the source directly in the conversation interface.

Each answer includes a **sources strip** below the message. Opening a contribution source displays a detailed side panel containing:

- Contributor display name
- Contributor type (`researcher`, `vendor`, `expert`)
- Landing page
- Declared expertise areas
- The exact content used by the AI

The displayed source depends on the contribution type:

| Contribution type | What the user sees |
| --- | --- |
| **Manual contribution — uploaded document** | Embedded document viewer showing the cited content directly inside the side panel. |
| **Manual contribution — article URL** | Link to the original article and rendered content preview. |
| **Automated contribution** | The exact portion of the API response used by the AI. |

Users can also provide positive or negative feedback directly from the source panel.

Contributor information is always rendered from Askeal’s database rather than generated by the AI to ensure reliable attribution and traceability.

---

## 3. What this means for contributors

- **Quality matters** — low-value, generic, unsafe, or overly promotional content is unlikely to be surfaced.
- **Metadata matters** — names, descriptions, categories, and expertise areas help Askeal retrieve contributions more accurately.
- **Updates are automatically reflected** — updating a contribution refreshes how it can be retrieved and surfaced.

If a contributor profile is disabled or deleted, previous citations remain visible in historical conversations, but no new answers will surface its contributions.
