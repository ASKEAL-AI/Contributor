# Contribution Quality Guidelines

Askeal is built to deliver reliable, explainable, and operational cybersecurity intelligence.

To maintain the quality and trustworthiness of answers provided to users, all contributions submitted to the platform must meet a high standard of relevance, accuracy, and technical value.

This document explains the types of contributions expected on Askeal, as well as the quality standards applied during the review process.

---

# What Askeal Accepts

Askeal welcomes contributions from cybersecurity vendors, researchers, independent experts, security teams, and organizations.

Examples of accepted contributions include:

- Cybersecurity research papers and technical reports
- Anonymized penetration testing reports
- Malware analysis and reverse engineering reports
- Vulnerability research and proof-of-concepts (PoCs)
- Threat intelligence content
- Detection rules and security playbooks
- Technical cybersecurity knowledge bases
- APIs exposing cybersecurity intelligence or capabilities
- Incident analysis and technical write-ups
- Threat actor tracking and campaign analysis
- Security advisories and remediation guidance
- Security tooling documentation and operational expertise

Contributions may be submitted manually (documents, links, structured content) or through APIs connected to Askeal.

---

# Contribution Quality Standards

All contributions are reviewed to ensure they provide meaningful and trustworthy value to users.

Contributions should:

- Provide technically accurate information
- Be based on real expertise or validated sources
- Contain actionable or operationally useful intelligence
- Be sufficiently detailed and contextualized
- Respect legal and ethical standards
- Avoid misleading, speculative, or unverifiable claims

Submissions that are incomplete, low-quality, promotional-only, duplicated, misleading, or unrelated to cybersecurity operations may require improvements before publication.

---

# What We Encourage

The most valuable contributions on Askeal typically:

- Help security teams make faster decisions
- Provide practical investigation or remediation guidance
- Add unique expertise or intelligence not easily accessible elsewhere
- Improve understanding of threats, vulnerabilities, or attacker behavior
- Deliver operational context rather than purely marketing content
- Include technical indicators, evidence, or reproducible analysis when relevant

---

# API Contributions

Contributors may also connect APIs exposing cybersecurity capabilities or intelligence.

Examples include:

- Threat intelligence APIs
- IOC enrichment services
- Malware analysis services
- Domain, IP, or URL reputation systems
- Security validation and investigation tools
- Vulnerability intelligence feeds
- Detection or exposure analysis systems

API integrations should provide reliable, stable, and security-relevant outputs.

Askeal may limit or disable integrations that consistently return low-quality, unreliable, inaccessible, or non-operational results.

---

# Content Restrictions

The following types of contributions are generally not accepted:

- Purely promotional or sales-oriented content
- Low-information or generic AI-generated content
- Misleading or unverifiable claims
- Duplicate submissions
- Illegal or unethical content
- Sensitive information shared without authorization
- Non-cybersecurity-related content
- Content designed primarily for SEO manipulation

---

# Review Process

Contributions may undergo a quality review before becoming available to users.

If a contribution does not yet meet Askeal quality standards, contributors may be invited to improve and resubmit their content.

Our objective is not only to maintain platform quality, but also to ensure that users can rely on Askeal for trustworthy and operational cybersecurity intelligence.

---

# Building Trusted Cybersecurity Intelligence Together

Askeal is designed as a collaborative cybersecurity intelligence ecosystem.

By contributing high-quality expertise, research, and operational knowledge, contributors help improve the reliability, diversity, and usefulness of cybersecurity intelligence accessible to professionals worldwide.
